Offshore Sportsbooks Betting FAQ

Do Offshore Sportsbooks Accept US Players?

Offshore betting sites do accept players from the majority of the US. There may be restricted States, so always make sure to look at the Terms and Conditions of the sportsbook to make sure.

What Is The Max Bet You Can Make At Offshore Betting Sites?

The max bet you can place at offshore sportsbooks averages between $500 and $2,000. Some sites may increase your wagering limits after a period of time or by request.

Can I Use Debit Or Credit Cards To Make A Deposit At Offshore Sportsbooks?

You can make deposits using Visa and MasterCard debit/credit cards at most offshore betting sites. There are a few websites that will accept other cards like Amex and Discover.

Are online sportsbooks legal in the US?

Yes, there is no federal law that criminalizes sports betting as an individual. Some states may have regulations against online gambling. However, these are not enforced or a priority for law enforcement.

Where is sports betting legal in the USA?

Oregon, Montana, Nevada, Colorado, New Mexico, Idaho, Illinois, Arkansas, Mississippi, Michigan, West Virginia, Pennsylvania, New York, New Jersey, Delware, New Hampshire, and Rhode Island all have some form of legalized and regulated sports betting.

Do online sportsbooks in the US pay out?

Yes, online sportsbooks have a variety of methods for deposits and withdrawals. They all have positives and negatives, but payouts are fast at the vast majority of sites.

What online sportsbooks accept PayPal in the US?

Betting sites in states that have regulated sports betting will accept PayPal. There are a lot of options for sportsbooks with PayPal or those with other methods.
